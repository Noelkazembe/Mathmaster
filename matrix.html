Matrices

A matrix is a rectangular array of numbers or expressions arranged in rows and columns.

Explanation

The order of a matrix represents the number of row or columns it has. The rows are listed first.
For example , matrix A is of 2 × 3, means it has 2 rows and 3 columns.

Criteria

  • Adding matrices
  • Subtracting matrices
  • Multiplying matrices
  • Dividing matrices

1. ADDITION OF MATRICES

$$ A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b\\ c & d \end{pmatrix} , B = \begin{pmatrix} e & f\\ g & h \end{pmatrix} $$ solve A + B

1. write the matrix as:

$$ = \begin{pmatrix} a & b\\ c & d \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} e & f\\ g & h \end{pmatrix} $$
2. Add the corresponding elements $$ = \begin{pmatrix} a + e & b + f\\ c + g & d + h \end{pmatrix} $$

Example1

Given that A = \(\begin{pmatrix} 3 & 7\\ 4 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\) and B = \(\begin{pmatrix} 6 & 1\\ 3 & 9 \end{pmatrix}\). Find A + B $$ = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 7\\ 4 & 2 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} 6 & 1\\ 3 & 9 \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{pmatrix} 3 + 6 & 7 + 1\\ 4 + 3 & 2 + 9 \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{pmatrix} 9 & 8\\ 7 & 11 \end{pmatrix} $$

Example 2

A = \(\begin{pmatrix} x & 4\\ 1 & 5 \end{pmatrix}\) , B = \(\begin{pmatrix} 8 & 3\\ 6 & y \end{pmatrix}\) and C = \(\begin{pmatrix} 10 & 7\\ 7 & 8 \end{pmatrix}\). Given that A + B = C. Find the value of x and y. $$ \begin{pmatrix} x & 4\\ 1 & 5 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 3\\ 6 & y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 10 & 7\\ 7 & 8 \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ \begin{pmatrix} x + 8 & 4 + 3\\ 1 + 6 & 5 + y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 10 & 7\\ 7 & 8 \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ \begin{pmatrix} x + 8 & 7\\ 7 & 5 + y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 10 & 7\\ 7 & 8 \end{pmatrix} $$ \( x + 8 = 10 \)
\(x = 10 - 8\)
\(x = 2\)

\(5 + y = 8\)
\(y = 8 - 5\)
\(y = 3\)


2. SUBTRACTION OF MATRICES

$$ A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b\\ c & d \end{pmatrix} , B = \begin{pmatrix} e & f\\ g & h \end{pmatrix} $$ solve A - B

1. write the matrix as:

$$ = \begin{pmatrix} a & b\\ c & d \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} e & f\\ g & h \end{pmatrix} $$
2. Subtract the corresponding elements $$ = \begin{pmatrix} a - e & b - f\\ c - g & d - h \end{pmatrix} $$

Example 1

Given that A = \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 7\\ 4 & 2\\ 4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\) and B = \(\begin{bmatrix} 6 & 1\\ 3 & 9\\ 5 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\). Find A - B $$ = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 7\\ 4 & 2\\ 4 & 8 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 1\\ 3 & 9\\ 5 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{bmatrix} 3 - 6 & 7 - 1\\ 4 - 3 & 2 - 9\\ 4 - 5 & 8 - 1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{bmatrix} -3& 6\\ 1 & - 7\\ -1 & 7 \end{bmatrix} $$

Example 2

Given that A = \(\begin{pmatrix} 8 & 7\\ 4 & 6 \end{pmatrix}\) and B = \(\begin{pmatrix} 3 & 1\\ 5 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\). Find A - B $$ = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 7\\ 4 & 6 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 1\\ 5 & 2 \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{pmatrix} 8 - 3 & 7 - 1\\ 4 - 5 & 6 - 2 \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & 6\\ - 1& 4 \end{pmatrix} $$

3. MULTIPLICATION OF MATRICES

a. Multiplying a matrix by a scalar

$$ k\begin{pmatrix} p & r\\ r & s \end{pmatrix} $$ Multiply each element by the scalar as $$ = \begin{pmatrix} kp & kq\\ kr & ks \end{pmatrix} $$

Example

Given that M = \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 6\\ 1 & - 4 \end{pmatrix}\). Calculate 3M. $$ = 3 \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 6\\ 1 & - 4 \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{pmatrix} 2 × 3 & 6 × 3\\ 1 × 3 & -4 × 3 \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{pmatrix} 6 & 18\\ 3 & - 12 \end{pmatrix} $$

b. Multiplying a matrix and a vector

NOTE: Number of columns in the matrix must be equal to the number of rows in the vector.

$$ \begin{bmatrix} a & c\\ b & d \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x\\ y \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{bmatrix} ax + dy\\ bx + dy \end{bmatrix} $$

Example 1

Solve \(\begin{bmatrix} 7 & 2\\ 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\) \(\begin{bmatrix} 4\\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\). $$ = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 2 \\ 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4\\ 1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{bmatrix} 7 × 4 + 2 × 1\\ 0 × 4 + 3 × 1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{bmatrix} 30\\ 3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Example 2

Find the values of x and y in the following matrix equation:
\(\begin{pmatrix} 6 & - 3 \\ 2 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\) \(\begin{pmatrix} x\\y \end{pmatrix}\) = \(\begin{pmatrix} 12\\ -4 \end{pmatrix}\) $$ \begin{pmatrix} 6x + (-3y)\\ 2x + 3y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 12\\ - 4 \end{pmatrix} $$ \(6x - 3y = 12\)......i
\(2x + 3y = -4\)......ii
Add equation (i) and equation (ii)
\(8x = 8\)
Devide both sides with 8.

\(x = 1\)
Substitute 1 for x.
\(2(1) + 3y = - 4\)
\(3y = -4 - 2\)
\(3y = - 6\)
Devide both sides with 3
\(\frac{3y}{3} = \frac{-6}{3}\)

\(y = - 2\)
\(x = 1\)

c. Multiplying a matrix and a matrix

NOTE: Number of columns in the matrix must be equal to the number of rows in the second matrix.

$$ \begin{pmatrix} a & b\\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} e & f\\ g & h \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ = \begin{pmatrix} ae + bg & af + bh\\ ce + dg & cf + dh \end{pmatrix} $$
